On average, residents in rural Iowa are older when compared to peope in urban parts of the state, according to the U.S. Census.

Median age: Rural: 42.4 Urban: 36.6

This means there is a higher concentration of older people in rural areas. These charts show the percentage of the population that falls within different age brackets.

Residents in rural areas also tend to make less money, according to the latest Census data.

Median household income: Rural $48,496 Urban $55,012

The percentage of the population under the poverty line is almost identical in rural and urban areas. The seven Census tracts with the highest percentages are urban areas.

Population under poverty line: Rural 12.1% Urban 12.7%

However, larger portion of seniors are living in poverty in rural Iowa. This chart defines seniors as residents 60 years of age or older.

Seniors under poverty line: Rural 15.7% Urban 12.8%
